Imagine a chef who tries to save time by guessing what ingredients you'll need next. He starts preparing them before you even finish your order. If his guess is wrong, he discards the prepped ingredients, but the bowls and utensils he used remain warm and rearranged.

Spectre works exactly like this: We trick the computer's "chef" (the CPU) into briefly accessing secret information during its guessing game. Even though it throws away the results, we can detect which "bowls" it used by measuring how warm they are - revealing the secret ingredients!

This attack doesn't break any locks or passwords - it simply observes the leftover clues from the computer's speed optimization techniques.
